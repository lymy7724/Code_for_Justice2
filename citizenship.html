Citizenship

Citizenship is a unique bond that unites people around civic ideals and a belief in the rights and freedoms guaranteed by the U.S. Constitution. There are many advantages to becoming a U.S. citizen including education, increased job opportunities, housing, family reunification, and voting in U.S. elections. Millions of lawful permanent residents are eligible to naturalize but need support to take the important steps of becoming a U.S. citizen.

REBL Resources provides the necessary support helping immigrants navigate the citizenship process. Deciding to become a U.S. citizen is a very important decision an immigrant can make. Depending on your situation, there are different ways to obtain citizenship. Below you can see the 7 steps of the naturalization application process.

If you are not a U.S. citizen by birth, or you did not acquire or derive U.S. citizenship from your parent(s) automatically after birth, go to the next step.

Review the naturalization eligibility worksheet to help you decide if you are eligible to apply for citizenship.

Prepare and Submit your Form N-400, Application for Naturalization

Once all the preliminary processes on your case are complete, USCIS will schedule an interview with you to complete the naturalization process. You must report to the USCIS office at the date and time on your appointment notice.

USCIS will mail a notice of decision to you. You will either be Granted, Continued, or Denied.

If USCIS Approves your N-400 in step 5, ou may be able to participate in a naturalization ceremony on the same day as your interview. If a same day naturalization ceremony is unavailable, USCIS will mail you a notification with the date, time, and location of your scheduled ceremony.

You are not a U.S. citizen until you take the Oath of Allegiance at a naturalization ceremony.
